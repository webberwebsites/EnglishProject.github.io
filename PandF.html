A Petrarchan Sonnet

While reading Lancelot and Guinevere;
Paolo and I felt the urge to kiss;
We betrayed God and all that he holds dear;
So we were sent to hell, that dark abyss;
Where the lustful feel agony and fear;
Dante came here, our pain he won’t dismiss;
Do not commit adultery don’t you dare;
Or else you will sin of incontinence.

We rot in hell and don’t have any hope;
Pain and agony has become our life;
All this for not listening to the Pope;
Now Dante can show the world what’s not right;
So he can tell us all to not elope.
